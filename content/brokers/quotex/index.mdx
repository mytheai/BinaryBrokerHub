## Regulation & Safety

**This is the section that should drive your decision.** If the regulatory situation does not satisfy you, stop reading and do not deposit.

### What license does Quotex actually hold?

The honest answer: **Quotex does not currently hold a valid financial license issued by any government regulator anywhere in the world.**

The only license Quotex's marketing materials and third-party broker directories cite is registration with the **International Financial Market Relations Regulation Center (IFMRRC)** under license number **TSRF RU 0395 AA V0161**, held by **Awesomo Limited**. Three problems with this:

1. **IFMRRC is not a government regulator.** It is a private self-regulatory body, headquartered in the Seychelles. It is not on the IOSCO membership list. It does not enforce dispute resolution outcomes, has no investor-compensation scheme, and does not have the legal authority to suspend a broker's operations.
2. **The license expired in 2021.** Multiple independent sources confirm that the IFMRRC registration of Awesomo Limited is no longer valid. Quotex has continued operating under this expired registration without obtaining a replacement license from any regulator.
3. **The operating-entity disclosure is inconsistent across sources.** Some directories list Awesomo Ltd as registered in the Seychelles. Others place the same entity in Saint Vincent and the Grenadines. A separate operating layer is reportedly **ON SPOT LLC GROUP**, registered in Saint Kitts and Nevis. The Quotex site itself does not consolidate these entities into a single transparent corporate-structure page.

This pattern — multiple legal entities across multiple offshore jurisdictions, a self-regulatory "license" that expired years ago and was never replaced, and an absence of any government regulator on the broker's authorisation page — is the **single most consequential YMYL fact for Quotex traders**. It means there is no ombudsman, no compensation scheme, and no enforcement mechanism if the broker chooses not to honour a withdrawal.

### Bonus and the turnover trap

Quotex periodically offers a **30% first-deposit bonus**. The bonus is **virtual** and cannot be withdrawn until specific turnover conditions are met. Quotex's exact turnover formula varies by promotion and is buried in the bonus terms — historical examples documented by third-party reviewers cite multipliers in the **x35–x40 range** of the bonus amount.

Concretely: if you accept a $30 bonus on a $100 deposit, you may face **$1,050–$1,200 of trade volume** before the bonus-derived funds become withdrawable. The math is structurally unfavourable to most retail accounts — the bonus turnover requirement is more likely to lock your balance than free it.

Quotex applies a **1% of daily balance fee (minimum $1/day) after 180 days of account inactivity**. This is uncommon among offshore binary brokers and easily missed in the terms. If you fund an account, trade for a week, and then forget about it for six months with $200 of remaining balance, the inactivity fee will erode your balance to zero within roughly 350 trading days at 1%/day or substantially faster if the $1/day floor binds. **Withdraw your balance fully or close the account if you stop using the platform.**

### Withdrawal — read this section twice

This is where Quotex's structural design diverges sharply from most competitors, and where the bulk of user complaints concentrate.

- **Minimum withdrawal**: ~$10 for bank transfer, ~$6 for card, ~$2.40 for e-wallets.
- **Advertised processing time**: Standard accounts 48 hours, Pro/VIP 24 hours, with up to 5 business days policy as the broker-side maximum. Crypto and e-wallet methods clear "near-instant" per advertised; bank transfer "hours or more."
- **Method-matching rule**: Quotex requires withdrawals to be made through the **same method used for the most recent deposit**. The implementation is not always intuitive — for Indian users, **a UPI deposit may force a bank-transfer withdrawal** rather than a UPI withdrawal back, because Quotex routes UPI inflows through P2P exchangers that do not support reverse flow. **Plan your deposit method based on your preferred withdrawal method, not the other way around.**
- **Reported reality (March–April 2026 surge)**: a notable spike in user reports of withdrawals showing status **"Successful"** on the platform but funds never arriving in the user's bank or wallet. Multiple users describe accounts being frozen at the moment of profit-withdrawal request, with KYC re-verification cycles extending the delay beyond the broker's 5-business-day policy. One Reddit case widely cited from this window: a user grew a balance from $100 to $810, requested a $110 withdrawal, was blocked, and ultimately had the account closed with only the original deposit returned.

If you do trade here, we recommend the following risk-mitigation steps:

1. **Verify your KYC fully before your first deposit.** Indian users in particular: complete Aadhaar/PAN verification immediately, do not let KYC become the post-profit excuse.
2. **Withdraw early and often.** Do not let a balance accumulate beyond what you would be willing to lose in a frozen-account scenario.
3. **Use crypto withdrawal where possible** — USDT TRC-20 has the fastest reported settlement and the fewest intermediary failure points. Card withdrawals at higher amounts have a worse track record across all offshore binary brokers, not just Quotex.
4. **Plan deposit method around withdrawal**, not the other way. UPI deposits in India may force bank-transfer withdrawals — if your bank account name does not match your KYC name exactly, this becomes a delay vector.
5. **Do not deposit more than you can absorb losing entirely.** This is the standard advice in the segment, and it applies in full at Quotex.
